A Royal Legacy

The story of Maheshwari fabric is inextricably linked to Queen Ahilyabai Holkar. In the 18th century, she invited master weavers from Surat and Mandu to settle in Maheshwar, tasking them with creating a fabric that embodied the grandeur of the town's architecture.

The result was a textile that mirrored the stone carvings of the fort, the rhythmic flow of the Narmada, and the geometric simplicity of the temple ghats.

The Art of Weaving

A meticulous symphony of silk and cotton, woven on traditional pit looms.

Traditional Technique

The fabric is traditionally woven using a silk warp and fine cotton weft, creating the perfect balance of sheen and comfort. Pure and tested zari threads are added to elevate the borders and motifs without overpowering the subtle elegance of the weave.

Dyeing and warping are done by hand, followed by a meticulous process of graphing motifs, drafting, denting and weaving on pit looms. Each fabric takes several days to complete.
