Maheshwari Weave

A timeless textile tradition from the historic town of Maheshwar, carrying the legacy of Queen Ahilyabai Holkar

Historic Legacy

Maheshwar, a historic town on the banks of the Narmada in Madhya Pradesh, is synonymous with one of India's most celebrated weaving traditions. The craft flourished in the 18th century under Queen Ahilyabai Holkar, who invited master weavers to develop textiles inspired by the architecture of Maheshwar Fort, temple carvings, and the rhythmic ghats.

The result was the Maheshwari weave — lightweight, lustrous and distinctive, carrying geometric motifs and reversible borders that became its signature.

The Art of Weaving

Traditional Process

The fabric is traditionally woven using a silk warp and fine cotton weft, creating the perfect balance of sheen and comfort. Pure and tested zari threads are added to elevate the borders and motifs without overpowering the subtle elegance of the weave.

Dyeing and warping are done by hand, followed by a meticulous process of graphing motifs, drafting, denting and weaving on pit looms. Each fabric takes several days to complete, with artisans maintaining precision in tension, structure and pattern.
